# Feng Shui Reference Guide for Room Planning

**TL;DR**
- This document organizes every major Feng Shui concept across three depth tiers (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced) and four schools (Form, BTB, Compass / Eight Mansions, Flying Stars / Xuan Kong Fei Xing) so a coding agent can apply room-level rules to any space — bedroom, living room, kitchen, bathroom, home office, dining room, entryway, children's room, meditation room, garage, basement.
- The single most important rule across all schools is the **Command Position** (bed, desk, stove, sofa: see the door, not in line with it, solid wall behind); the second is **clutter removal**; the third is the **Five Elements weakening cycle** (preferred over destructive) for nearly all imbalance cures.
- For 2024–2044 (Period 9), update default guidance: real water now belongs in the **North**, a mountain/heavy mass in the **South**, and the **#9 Purple Fire star** dominates wealth/health energies; legacy rules from Period 8 (water in SE) should be flagged.

> **How to use this document.** Each rule has a natural-language statement and a `Metadata` line of key–value tags an agent can index on (`tier`, `category`, `rule_type`, `room_applicability`, `element`, `direction`, `bagua_zone`, `polarity`, `cure`). Conflicts between schools are explicit; if both BTB and Compass apply, pick one system per consultation and stay consistent. Feng Shui's outcomes are about *reducing spatial friction* and aligning attention with intention — present them as guidelines, not magical guarantees.

## Key Findings

1. **BTB ≠ Compass.** BTB / Black Sect Tantric Buddhism Feng Shui, founded by H.H. Grandmaster Professor Thomas Lin Yun (per Yun Lin Temple's official site: *"H.H. Lin Yun first introduced the Black Sect Esoteric Buddhism Feng Shui to the United States in the early 1970s,"* with **Yun Lin Temple founded in Berkeley, California in 1986** as "the first reformist Black Sect Esoteric Buddhist temple at its fourth stage in the West"), aligns the Bagua to the **front-door wall** (Gen–Kan–Qian row), ignoring magnetic direction. Classical Compass School aligns by **Luo Pan compass**. They give different "Wealth corners" for the same room — choose one system per consultation.
2. **The four Eight Mansions auspicious directions and their canonical strength ranks are universal across sources:** Sheng Chi (生氣, wealth, ~100%), Yan Nian (延年, love/longevity, ~90%), Tien Yi (天醫, health, ~75%), Fu Wei (伏位, stability, ~65%). The four inauspicious: Huo Hai (~50%), Liu Sha (~40%), Wu Gui (~25%), Jue Ming (~10%, worst).
3. **Period 9 (Feb 4, 2024 – Feb 3, 2044)** is now in effect. The Feng Shui Store (fengshuiweb.co.uk) is explicit: *"the basic rules for period 9 2024–2044 are you must have real water, road or lower ground in the north and a mountain, building, trees or high fence in the south."* This inverts much Period 8 (2004–2023) advice. Star shifts: per World of Feng Shui (wofs.com), *"The Black Star 2, typically a star to be feared, becomes less malevolent"* in Period 9; Star 9 (Purple/Fire) reigns; Star 4 enters a transitional state (per fengshui.net: *"3-9/4-9…Leave it alone, don't do anything"*).
4. **Five Elements remediation should default to the weakening cycle, not the destructive cycle.** Per FengShuiSource.com (Master Xion): *"A gentler approach is to use the Weakening Cycle…The weakening approach often works better for lasting harmony."* Destructive cures (e.g., water on fire) often backfire (steam, smoke, blowback).
5. **Material > Shape > Color** when reading any object's element. A metal-bodied lamp with a red shade reads as Metal, not Fire.

## Details

### 1. Chi / Qi Flow

Chi (氣) is life energy. Smooth meandering chi (**sheng chi**) nourishes; fast straight-line or stagnant chi (**sha chi** / "killing energy" or "poison arrow chi") harms. The front door is the **"Mouth of Chi"** — every room's chi quality starts there.

- **1.1 Beginner** Keep pathways through every room unobstructed. `Metadata: {tier:beginner, category:chi_flow, rule_type:placement, room:all}`
- **1.2 Beginner** Break long straight sightlines (front door → back door or window) with a screen, console, rug, or plant. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:avoidance, room:entryway/hallway}`
- **1.3 Beginner** Clutter creates stagnant chi; every room needs clear floor space. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:avoidance, room:all}`
- **1.4 Intermediate** Chi enters via doors/windows and exits the same way. Use rugs, furniture groupings, and curves to slow it before exit. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:placement}`
- **1.5 Intermediate** Yang chi dominates active rooms (kitchen, office, living room); Yin chi dominates rest rooms (bedroom, meditation). `{tier:intermediate, polarity:yin_yang}`
- **1.6 Advanced** In Period 9, place real water/low ground in the **North** and heavy mass/buildings/trees in the **South** to align with the era's dominant qi pattern. `{tier:advanced, period:9, direction:N/S}`

### 3. Five Elements Theory (Wu Xing)

| Element | Shape | Colors | Materials | Direction | Season |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wood** | Rectangle, columnar | Green, brown, teal | Wood, bamboo, paper, plants | E, SE | Spring |
| **Fire** | Triangle, pointed, star | Red, orange, hot pink, purple | Candles, lights, leather, wool | S | Summer |
| **Earth** | Square, flat, horizontal | Yellow, beige, terracotta | Ceramic, stone, clay, brick, tile | SW, NE, Center | Late summer / transitions |
| **Metal** | Circle, oval, arch | White, gray, silver, gold | Iron, steel, copper, brass, rocks | W, NW | Autumn |
| **Water** | Wavy, asymmetric, freeform | Black, dark blue, charcoal | Glass, mirror, real water | N | Winter |

**Three cycles:**
- **Productive (Sheng):** Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood. To strengthen an element, add its "mother."
- **Destructive (Ke):** Wood breaks Earth, Earth dams Water, Water douses Fire, Fire melts Metal, Metal cuts Wood. Use cautiously to subdue excess (can backfire — e.g., water on grease fire creates steam/explosion).
- **Weakening (Xie):** Fire weakens Wood, Earth weakens Fire, Metal weakens Earth, Water weakens Metal, Wood weakens Water. **Preferred gentle remedy**; per FengShuiSource.com (Master Xion): *"A gentler approach is to use the Weakening Cycle…The weakening approach often works better for lasting harmony."*

- **3.1 Intermediate** In each bagua zone, the native element predominates; the "mother" element is secondary; the destructive element stays ≤10%. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:balance}`
- **3.2 Intermediate** Read element of an object by **Material > Shape > Color** in that priority order (Feng Shui Balanz). `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:prioritization}`
- **3.3 Advanced** When an element is excessive, prefer weakening over destructive cycle. Example: excess Water in West (mirrored closet) → add Earth (ceramics, square rug, beige), not a Fire clash. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:remediation}`
- **3.4 Advanced** Mix at least three of five elements per room; mono-element rooms (e.g., all-white Metal kitchens) feel cold and energetically depleted. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:balance}`

### 4. Yin and Yang Balance

Yin = passive, dark, cool, soft, quiet, curved, low. Yang = active, bright, warm, hard, loud, angular, tall.

| Room | Target | Practical |
|---|---|---|
| Bedroom | ~70% Yin | Soft light, layered fabrics, muted colors, curves |
| Meditation room | ~80% Yin | Natural materials, dim light, minimal decor |
| Bathroom | Balanced | Counter Water (Yin) with Earth/Wood |
| Living room | ~50/50 | Soft sofas + structured tables, bold art |
| Dining room | Slightly Yang | Bright but warm; conversation-friendly |
| Kitchen | Yang-leaning | Light, fire, activity; balance with stone/wood |
| Home office | ~70% Yang | Bright light, clean lines + one Yin Yin accent |
| Children's room | Balanced; Yin at night | Pastels; dimmable lighting |
| Entryway | Yang | Bright and welcoming |
| Basement | Often too Yin | Add Yang lights, mirrors, plants |
| Garage | Yang/transitional | Tidy; ground rooms above |

- **4.1 Beginner** Bedrooms must lean Yin; remove TVs, exercise gear, work desks. `{tier:beginner, polarity:yin, room:bedroom}`
- **4.2 Beginner** Active rooms must have adequate light; dark active rooms breed stagnation. `{tier:beginner, polarity:yang}`
- **4.3 Intermediate** Diagnose room imbalance first by Yin/Yang: too sleepy → add Yang; too restless → add Yin. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:diagnosis}`

### 5. Command Position

Apply to **bed, desk, stove, primary sofa, and any chair used >1 hour daily**.

- **5.1 Beginner** Place the piece diagonally farthest from the door, sees the door, not directly in line with it. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:placement, room:bedroom/office/kitchen/living_room}`
- **5.2 Beginner** Solid wall (Tortoise) backs the piece. No back to door, window, or open hallway. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:placement}`
- **5.3 Beginner** A bed with feet pointing out the door is the "coffin position" / "dead man's position." Avoid. If unavoidable: add substantial footboard, bench at foot of bed, or screen. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:avoidance, room:bedroom}`
- **5.4 Beginner** When Command Position is impossible, angle a small mirror so the user can see the door from where they sit/sleep. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:cure, cure:mirror}`
- **5.5 Intermediate** Priority order if compromising: (1) see the door, (2) not in line with door, (3) solid wall behind. Sacrifice #3 first; heavy curtains substitute. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:prioritization}`
- **5.6 Advanced** Stove Command Position: cook sees kitchen entrance while cooking; stove does not face toilet, sink, fridge, or window directly. `{tier:advanced, room:kitchen}`

### 7. Eight Mansions (Ba Zhai) — Kua Numbers

**Kua calculation** (use solar / Li Chun calendar; if born before ~Feb 4, use previous year):
- Male: sum last two digits of birth year → reduce to single digit → subtract from 10. (Kua 5 → use 2.)
- Female: sum last two digits → reduce → add 5. (Kua 5 → use 8.)
- For births 2000+, many practitioners adjust: male subtracts from 9; female adds 6.

**Groups:**
- **East Group (Kua 1, 3, 4, 9)** — auspicious: N, S, E, SE
- **West Group (Kua 2, 6, 7, 8)** — auspicious: W, NW, SW, NE

**Four Auspicious (Wandering Stars):**

| Name | Translation | Element | Governs | Application |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sheng Chi (生氣)** | Generating Breath | Yang Wood | Wealth, career (~100%) | Face while working; front door faces |

| **Yan Nian (延年)** | Prolonged Years | Yang Metal | Longevity, love, relationships (~90%) | Head of bed for couples; dining |
| **Fu Wei (伏位)** | Stability / Sitting | Yin Wood | Personal growth, study, meditation (~65%) | Meditation seat; young children's beds |

**Four Inauspicious:**

### 8. Flying Stars (Xuan Kong Fei Xing)

Each building has a "natal" chart based on its construction Period and facing direction. Three star types fly through 9 palaces: **Mountain Star** (health/relationships), **Water Star** (wealth/career), **Time Star** (period/annual/monthly).

**Periods:** P1 1864–1883 · P2 1884–1903 · P3 1904–1923 · P4 1924–1943 · P5 1944–1963 · P6 1964–1983 · P7 1984–2003 · P8 2004–2023 · **P9 Feb 4, 2024 – Feb 3, 2044** · P1 2044–2063.

**Period 9 Highlights:**
- **Star 9 (Purple Fire)** reigns — peaks in the **South**.
- **Star 8 (White Earth)** is "departed" but retains residual positive influence.
- **Star 1 (White Water)** is the future-leaning star.
- **Star 2** transitions favorably: per World of Feng Shui (wofs.com), *"The Black Star 2, typically a star to be feared, becomes less malevolent"* in Period 9 — though still requires careful handling at the start of the period.
- **Star 4** enters a transitional state in Period 9; per fengshui.net guidance on the 3-9 / 4-9 combinations: *"Leave it alone, don't do anything,"* indicating neither clear inauspiciousness nor full auspiciousness — treat as neutral and watch year-by-year.
- **Period 9 baseline rule** (Feng Shui Store, fengshuiweb.co.uk): *"the basic rules for period 9 2024–2044 are you must have real water, road or lower ground in the north and a mountain, building, trees or high fence in the south."*
- **2026 specifics:** Star 1 in center (new beginnings); South hosts Star 5 (Misfortune) + Tai Sui — calm with metal (six-rod wind chime), no construction, no red/yellow; Southeast hosts Star 9 (best 2026 sector) — activate with water, bright light, healthy plants, purple/red accents.

- **8.1 Advanced** Luo Pan compass reading within ±3° is required; small errors shift palace allocations. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:measurement}`
- **8.2 Advanced** Treat Period stars as 20-year strategy; Annual stars (rolling Feb 4) for yearly tuning; Monthly for fine timing of launches/contracts. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:methodology}`
- **8.3 Advanced** When Annual #5 (Yellow) lands in a critical room, apply metal element (six-rod wind chime, brass) to weaken Earth — never fire/red. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:remediation}`
- **8.4 Advanced** Do not place water features in the South during Period 9 (Water douses #9 Fire). North is the favored water placement. `{tier:advanced, period:9, direction:N}`

### 12. Lighting

- **12.1 Beginner** Layered lighting ("3-light rule"): ≥3 sources per room — overhead + floor + table lamp. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:placement}`
- **12.2 Beginner** Replace burnt-out bulbs immediately — "dead" light = stagnation. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:maintenance}`
- **12.3 Beginner** Bedrooms: warm yellow, dimmable, low intensity. No heavy fixture or ceiling fan directly over the bed. `{tier:beginner, room:bedroom}`
- **12.4 Intermediate** Activate dark stagnant corners with a lamp (Yang cure). Crystal chandeliers auspicious in foyers — speed chi, refract light. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:enhancement}`
- **12.5 Beginner** Soften bare bulbs with shades; harsh "hospital lighting" generates sha chi. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:avoidance}`
- **12.6 Beginner** Maximize daylight; open curtains during the day. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:enhancement}`

### 13. Mirrors

Mirrors are Water-element amplifiers. Whatever they reflect is doubled.

- **13.1 Beginner** Never directly face the bed (sleep disruption; traditionally said to invite "third parties" / restless energy). `{tier:beginner, room:bedroom, rule_type:avoidance}`
- **13.2 Beginner** Never on the wall directly facing the front door (bounces incoming chi back out). Place perpendicular instead. `{tier:beginner, room:entryway}`
- **13.3 Beginner** Mirrors must reflect something beautiful/intentional: view, plant, dining table, art. Avoid reflecting clutter, toilet, stove, or bed. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:placement}`
- **13.4 Beginner** Dining room mirror reflecting the table doubles abundance (food = wealth). `{tier:beginner, room:dining_room, rule_type:enhancement}`
- **13.5 Beginner** Never reflect the kitchen stove (excess Fire, accident risk). `{tier:beginner, room:kitchen, rule_type:avoidance}`
- **13.6 Beginner** No mirrors on bedroom ceilings or above beds. `{tier:beginner, room:bedroom, rule_type:avoidance}`
- **13.7 Intermediate** Shape: round/oval encourages flow; square/rectangular grounds. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:shape}`
- **13.8 Intermediate** Replace cracked, aged, or distorted mirrors immediately. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:maintenance}`
- **13.9 Advanced** Convex Bagua mirrors are **outdoor** deflection devices (against cemetery, hospital, T-intersection sha). Never hang inside the home. Concave Bagua mirrors absorb. `{tier:advanced, rule_type:cure}`
- **13.10 Beginner** Treat TVs like mirrors when off; don't position screens facing the bed. `{tier:beginner, room:bedroom, rule_type:avoidance}`

### 14. Plants

- **14.1 Beginner** Use healthy plants with soft, rounded, heart-shaped leaves (jade, money tree, pothos, peace lily, philodendron, areca palm). Remove dead/dying plants immediately. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:selection}`
- **14.2 Beginner** Avoid spiky/thorny plants (cacti, dragon trees, agave) in living areas, bedrooms, near seating. `{tier:beginner, rule_type:avoidance}`
- **14.3 Beginner** Traditional rule: no plants in the bedroom (Wood chi competes with rest). Modern view (Anjie Cho): small healthy non-spiky plants are acceptable if not crowding the bed. `{tier:beginner, room:bedroom}`
- **14.4 Beginner** Plants in bathrooms: traditional view avoids them ("grow in waste"); modern view permits a small plant in NE/SW/center bathrooms to drain affliction. `{tier:beginner, room:bathroom}`
- **14.5 Intermediate** Place plants in Wood zones (E/Family, SE/Wealth). Avoid large plants/groupings in Earth zones (SW, NE, Center) — Wood drains Earth and may affect health/relationships. `{tier:intermediate, direction:E/SE, rule_type:enhancement}`
- **14.6 Intermediate** Lucky bamboo numerology: 2 stalks=love, 3=happiness, 5=health, 6=wealth, 8=abundance, 9=fortune. Avoid 4 stalks. `{tier:intermediate, rule_type:symbolism}`
- **14.7 Intermediate** Tall plant in front of a sharp corner = canonical poison-arrow cure. `{tier:intermediate, cure:poison_arrow}`
- **14.8 Beginner** Pair plants on either side of the front door for symmetry + Wood/health energy. `{tier:beginner, room:entryway, rule_type:enhancement}`

### 20. Room-Specific Consolidated Rules

#### 20.1 Bedroom
Yin-dominant; rest; intimacy. Command Position bed; solid headboard; both sides accessible with paired bedside tables/lamps. No mirrors facing bed (cover at night or place inside closet); no TV facing bed; no exposed beam over bed; no sloped ceiling at head; no bed directly above downstairs stove/kitchen/toilet (causes health issues to the body part above the appliance); no work desk in bedroom (separate with screen if unavoidable); no exercise equipment; no water features; ≤1–2 small healthy non-spiky plants (or none); no cacti; soft Yin lighting; no heavy fixture over bed; under-bed empty (or soft linens only); soft pastels/skin tones; no bold reds. Advanced: head of bed points to sleeper's Tien Yi (health) or Yan Nian (love).

#### 20.2 Living Room
Balanced Yin/Yang. Sofa in Command Position (solid wall behind, sees entrance, not in line with front door); U- or L-shape seating; round/oval coffee table; sofa not under exposed beam; center of room (Tai Chi) open; one focal point (not solely TV); plants in corners; mirror reflects view/art/room, not entrance or seating; activate back-left Wealth corner (BTB) with plant, lamp, or aquarium; no sofa back to window.

#### 20.3 Kitchen
Stove in modified Command Position — cook sees entrance; if impossible, small angled mirror reflects entrance for cook's peripheral view (but never reflects burners). Stove never faces toilet, fridge, sink directly; not between two water elements (sink AND fridge flanking); not under upstairs bathroom; not under a window; fridge/stove apart; knives stored away; no mirror reflecting stove; multi-burner preferred (= multiple income sources); avoid kitchen location in SW (Mother's luck) or NW (Father's luck); kitchen not visible directly from front door; not all-green (excess Wood feeds Fire excessively).

#### 20.4 Bathroom
Toilet lid down; door closed; toilet not facing bathroom door directly; bathroom not adjacent to or above kitchen stove, front door, or bed; best locations NE (some schools); add Earth tones to balance Water; small plant in NE/SW/center bathroom drains affliction; mirror never reflects toilet; round/oval mirror preferred; fix leaks immediately; good Yang lighting.

#### 20.5 Home Office
Yang-dominant. Desk in Command Position; if facing wall, hang expansive horizon art ("symbolic window") or angled mirror to see door. Avoid desk facing window directly (chi/focus leak — mitigate with curtains or windowsill plant). Apply BTB Bagua to desktop: back-left Wealth (plant/lamp), back-center Fame (nameplate), back-right Love (paired objects/flower), front-center Career (computer/black object), front-right Helpful People, front-left Knowledge (books), center Health (clear). "Dragon moves, Tiger stays" — active devices on left, static on right. High-backed chair with arms. No exposed beam. No work desk in bedroom. Advanced: desk faces user's Sheng Chi or Fu Wei.

#### 20.6 Dining Room
Round/oval/rounded-corner table; size proportional to room; mirror reflects table (doubles abundance) but not the front door; head of household in Tortoise position (back to wall, facing entrance); fresh fruit display (oranges, apples, pears); not under upstairs bathroom; not facing front door directly; no sharp ceiling beam or chandelier corners over table; layered warm lighting. Advanced: head of household faces breadwinner's Tien Yi or Yan Nian.

#### 20.7 Entryway / Foyer
Bright lighting inside and out; front door functional, grandest in home; welcome mat clean and proportional; use front door daily; color matched to facing direction; clear ming tang (open landing) just past door; if entry faces a wall, hang horizontal bidirectional art (not a mirror); mirror perpendicular to door, not opposite; pair of plants flanking door; shoes stored in closed cabinet (not scattered); console table with closed storage; no door-blocking furniture; convex Bagua mirror outside if facing cemetery, T-intersection, hospital, or sharp roofline; stairs facing door cured with rug/plant/crystal.

#### 20.8 Children's Room
Command Position bed (under ~12: bed against one wall AND headboard against wall = "den" security; over ~12: standard Command Position); bed not under window or sloped ceiling at head; bunk beds not ideal (compress upper child's chi) — if used: wood not metal, paint underside of upper bunk soothing color; bedroom not above garage (shifting energy — add grounding furniture, exterior vines/tree mural cure); not above basement electrical panels (EMR risk); soft pastel walls (per Anjie Cho — blues, greens, pinks); family/parent photos IN the room (security, authority); avoid wild animal/predator/violent imagery; full-length mirror at child's eye level boosts shy child's confidence; no mirror facing bed; no fountain; no spiky plants; limit electronics; phone/tablet removed at night; child's room not farther back/higher than master bedroom (inverts authority hierarchy).

Maximum Yin. Soft natural light, dimmable; candles welcome (Fire balance); natural materials (wood, cotton, linen, stone — minimize plastics/metal); earth tones with optional NE Bagua (Knowledge) accents; heavy grounding object (stone, statue, salt lamp); minimal plants (round leaves only); cushion in user's Fu Wei direction if Kua known; no mirrors; no electronics; no water features (sound too active for deep meditation); optional small altar with intentional symbolic objects.

Park nose-out (ready to leave smoothly); functional garage door, no squeaks; toxic chemicals stored away from walls shared with bedrooms; clutter in closed shelving; if a bedroom/living room is above the garage: add grounding (heavy furniture, area rug) upstairs and consider exterior vine plantings or tree mural as symbolic cure; move car out quickly after starting (exhaust drains air); no bedroom directly above the garage door.

#### 20.11 Basement
Often too Yin. Bright Yang lighting (no dim single bulb); mirrors to reflect light; plants (with grow-light); dehumidify (mold = severe sha); avoid basement bedrooms if possible — if unavoidable, red accents, full-spectrum light, Command Position bed, air purifier; declutter quarterly; storage by category in closed bins; no electrical panels under occupied beds upstairs.

- **Poison Arrow / Sha Chi (煞氣)** — Sharp cutting energy from corners, beams, T-intersections aimed at occupants.
- **Sheng Chi (生氣)** — Generating breath; also the strongest auspicious Kua direction.

## Recommendations

For an AI agent guiding users:

1. **Always tier the user first.** Ask three gate questions:
   - Do you know your Kua number, or have your birth year + gender? *(unlocks Advanced)*
   - Do you have a compass app and know your home's facing direction (within ±3°)? *(unlocks Advanced)*
   - Do you want practical/Western-style or traditional/Eastern guidance? *(determines BTB vs Compass)*
   - **Threshold to upgrade to Intermediate:** the user understands the Five Elements list.
   - **Threshold to upgrade to Advanced:** the user knows their Kua AND has a compass reading.

2. **Default to BTB Three-Door at Beginner/Intermediate.** It requires no compass and applies universally. Switch to Compass School only when the user explicitly opts in with a compass reading.

3. **Apply the rules in this priority order when they conflict** (the agent should not violate higher items to satisfy lower ones):
   1. Safety / building code
   2. Command Position for bed/desk/stove/sofa
   3. Declutter
   4. Yin/Yang balance for the room's function
   5. Five Elements balance in the bagua zone
   6. Poison arrow cures
   7. Color and shape alignment
   8. Kua direction (Advanced only)
   9. Flying Star annual cure (Advanced only)

4. **For cures, default to the Weakening cycle** (Master Xion: *"the weakening approach often works better for lasting harmony"*), not the Destructive cycle. Use Destructive only when an element is so dominant that gentler measures cannot dent it.

5. **Update legacy Period 8 guidance.** In any rule referencing "fountain in SE for wealth," append: *"Period 8 rule (2004–2023); for Period 9 (2024–2044), real water now belongs in the North per fengshuiweb.co.uk."*

6. **Hedge symbolic outcomes.** Use language like "traditionally said to," "symbolically associated with," "many practitioners report" — Feng Shui's measurable effect is on attention, sleep, prospect-and-refuge psychology, and spatial friction; outcomes are not deterministic.

7. **Defer to occupant feedback.** If a "correctly" Feng Shui'd arrangement feels wrong to the inhabitant, the practice is failing its purpose. Adjust toward subjective comfort.

8. **Benchmarks that change recommendations:**
   - Move to a new home OR major renovation after Feb 4, 2044 → recalculate as Period 1 home.
   - User has new significant other / partner moves in → recheck Kua compatibility and bed direction.
   - User reports sleep disturbance, recurring illness, or financial stagnation → audit Command Position, mirror placement, and clutter under bed/in closet of relevant Kua-direction sector first.
   - Annual Flying Star changes each Feb 4 → re-evaluate sector cures for the year (especially Star 5 / Tai Sui location).

## Caveats

1. **Feng Shui schools genuinely disagree.** BTB practitioners and Classical/Compass practitioners give different answers for the same room. Critics of BTB (Kartar Diamond, FengShuiBalanz) argue it is a Western New-Age simplification developed by Lin Yun in the 1970s–80s and is inconsistent with classical Form / Compass / Flying Star Feng Shui. Critics of Compass School argue it's "fear-based" and overly rigid. Both views are partisan; the right call is to pick one system per user and stay consistent.

2. **Eight Mansions has its own internal controversy.** Per Feng Shui Balanz, master Jiang Da Hong argued historically that Ba Zhai is fundamentally flawed because it treats Qi as static, while the Flying Star system treats Qi as dynamic. Some traditional sources suggest the male/female split in Kua calculation was a deliberate "decoy" planted in the Eight Mansion Bright Mirror text to mislead enemies of the Tang emperor. Use Eight Mansions as one input among several, not as a sole determinant.

3. **Many "rules" are aesthetic conventions, not metaphysics.** Avoiding clutter, balancing light, using rounded furniture corners, placing the bed where you can see the door — all map cleanly onto modern environmental psychology (prospect–refuge theory, sleep hygiene, sensory load). Present these as design-with-purpose, not as causal magic.

4. **Outdated rules from pre-modern buildings.** Per FengShuiNexus, several traditional rules (no kitchen in West sector for spoilage; toilet directly above stove) assumed pre-refrigeration, pre-plumbing buildings. Modern equivalents need re-evaluation. The rule that survives best is *symbolic* (fire under water = career setbacks), but should be hedged.

5. **Cultural specificity of numbers.** The "unlucky 4" rule derives from Mandarin/Cantonese homophone with 死 ("death") and does not transfer to other languages. For Western users, Carol Olmstead's reframe — "4 sounds like 'more'" — is a reasonable accommodation. Do not impose the 4-avoidance rule on users whose address contains 4 if they have no Chinese-language association with the number.

6. **Period 9 is recent (started Feb 4, 2024); guidance is still being calibrated.** Some 81-combination interpretations and annual cures are evolving as the period unfolds. Treat Period 9 specifics as best-current-consensus, not settled doctrine, especially regarding Star 2 (becoming auspicious but still volatile per WOFS) and Star 4 (transitional per fengshui.net).

7. **Mirror practice diverges sharply between schools.** Some BTB practitioners use mirrors more freely (as "minor adjustments" / xie zi fa); some Classical practitioners restrict mirrors to specific Bagua mirror cures. The conservative default in this document is the Classical view.

8. **Health and life outcomes are not guaranteed.** No Feng Shui adjustment substitutes for medical advice, financial planning, or relationship counseling. Position this practice as one supportive lever among many.

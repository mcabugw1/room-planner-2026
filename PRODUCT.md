# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Homeowners and renters planning real rooms. Their context: sitting at home, trying to figure out if furniture fits or how to rearrange a space before moving things. Non-technical, practical goal — they want a clear answer, not a design tool.

## Product Purpose

A practical web tool for interactive room layout planning. Place and arrange furniture on a to-scale floor plan, add wall features (windows, doors), and get a confident spatial picture before committing to a real rearrangement.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, helpful. The tool should feel like a trusted tape measure — dependable, no noise, gets out of the way. Approachable to non-technical users without being patronizing.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS blue (#0066ff everywhere, Bootstrap vibes)
- Heavy dark mode (all-black background, neon accents)
- Real estate / listing sites (photo-heavy, marketing copy, price cards)

## Design Principles

1. **Canvas first.** The room canvas is the product. Controls are supporting cast — useful but never competing.
2. **Calm precision.** Every number and label should feel accurate and trustworthy. No decorative clutter around functional elements.
3. **Warm but not warm-fuzzy.** Take warmth cues from IKEA Place and Houzz — approachable, residential-feeling — without tipping into consumer-app candy or real-estate marketing.
4. **One task at a time.** The sidebar guides a clear sequence. Don't make users hunt or parse.
5. **Honest materials.** The visual language should echo real-world planning: paper, pencil, floor plans. Grounded, not gamified.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Good contrast, keyboard navigability, no major fails. Practical bar, not formally audited.
